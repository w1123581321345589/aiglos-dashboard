02 — The Pricing Problem

Usage-Based Pricing Kills Supernovas

The original Aiglos pricing model — $0.001 per tool call, $0.01 per block, $0.05 per attestation — is rational for extracting value. It is wrong for building a Supernova. The redesigned model keeps the viral free tier but compresses the time between free and paid using tight feature gates and a 30-day trial clock.

The Redesigned Model: Seed Fast, Convert Faster

The free tier remains the distribution engine — local-only, unlimited tool call inspection, zero friction to install. But the gates are tighter now. Attestation artifacts are locked at zero on the free tier. Cloud telemetry is locked. The moment a developer tries either, they hit a persistent but non-blocking upgrade prompt.

Then the 30-day Pro trial fires automatically. The developer experiences the full product — cloud dashboard, signed artifacts, compliance PDF — before they hit the wall. By day 30 they already know exactly what they lose if they don't convert.

Cursor converted at 36% (vs 2–5% industry average) because the product was undeniably better before the paywall. Same principle here: let developers discover the value of attestation artifacts during the trial, then make the ask.

Usage-based pricing stays inside the enterprise and DoD tiers where procurement conversations already involve math. The bottom of the funnel is flat and frictionless. The trial is the conversion mechanism — not a demo, not a sales call, not a free tier that gives away the product forever.
